# Global Store Retail Performance & Profitability Analysis - Business Case

## Business Context

Global Store operates across multiple markets, regions, product categories, and customer segments. The company generates strong sales volume, but management needs to understand whether that sales volume is translating into profitable growth.

The analysis focuses on a practical business risk: revenue can increase while margin quality weakens because of discounting, product-level losses, shipping cost, and regional performance gaps.

## Core Business Question

> Where is Global Store generating profitable growth, and where is revenue being created without enough profit?

## Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | What They Need From the Analysis |
| --- | --- |
| Retail leadership | Overall sales, profit, margin, and growth quality |
| Category managers | Product categories and sub-categories that drive or destroy profit |
| Sales managers | Strong regions, weak regions, and segment opportunities |
| Operations team | Shipping cost, delivery days, and priority patterns |
| BI / analytics team | Reproducible data pipeline, SQL logic, and dashboard outputs |

## Business Questions Answered

| Area | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Executive KPIs | What are total sales, profit, orders, customers, quantity, and margin? |
| Sales performance | Which categories, products, markets, and regions generate the most revenue? |
| Profitability | Which products and sub-categories create margin leakage? |
| Discounting | How does discount level affect profit margin? |
| Customers | Which customer segments and customers contribute most to revenue and profit? |
| Shipping | Which ship modes and priorities carry the highest cost? |
| Seasonality | Which months and quarters show stronger sales and profit patterns? |

## Key Findings

| Finding | Business Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Technology is the strongest category by revenue and profit | Technology can support growth without weakening margin quality |
| Tables is a loss-making sub-category | High revenue does not guarantee profitability; pricing and cost control are needed |
| Discounts above 30% are linked with negative margin | Discount policy needs stronger control rules |
| Southeast Asia has high sales but weak profit | Regional growth should be reviewed before increasing investment |
| Consumer drives scale; Home Office has stronger margin | Segment strategy should separate revenue growth from margin growth |

## Recommended Actions

| Priority | Action | Expected Business Value |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 1 | Review Tables pricing, supplier cost, shipping cost, and discounting | Reduce recurring loss from a high-sales sub-category |
| 2 | Create approval rules for discounts above 30% | Lower the risk of unprofitable sales |
| 3 | Investigate Southeast Asia product mix and cost drivers | Improve regional profit quality before scaling further |
| 4 | Prioritize profitable Technology products | Grow sales while protecting margin |
| 5 | Monitor sales and margin together in Power BI | Avoid decisions based only on revenue volume |

## Scenario-Based Impact Notes

The business impact statements in this project are scenario-based. They are not measured post-implementation results.

Examples:

- Reducing excessive discounts could improve gross margin if sales volume remains stable.
- Reducing losses in the Tables sub-category could improve category-level profitability.
- Improving Southeast Asia product mix, discounting, or shipping cost could turn high revenue into stronger profit contribution.
